Lincoln Access Screen Reader

A screen reader–style accessibility companion designed for K–12 and state school settings—built to align with how schools actually deploy software and protect student data.

What this is

This project is an independent effort inspired by the goals of open tools like NVDA: reliable spoken access to what is on screen, keyboard-driven workflows, and respect for users who rely on assistive technology. It is not NVDA itself; it is a separate application and website meant to support similar outcomes in environments where district policy, hosting, and privacy law matter as much as features do.

Why I built it

Schools need assistive technology that fits their reality: clear data practices, sensible deployment options, and alignment with state rules such as the Illinois Student Online Personal Protection Act (SOPPA) and comparable frameworks. Commercial and consumer tools do not always map cleanly onto those requirements.

I started this project to explore a SOPPA-conscious path: minimize unnecessary collection of student information, keep the trust boundary obvious, and make the product something a school can link to, evaluate, and stand behind—while staying focused on real accessibility needs in the classroom and at home.
